# Domain evidence guide

Read the relevant section before researching a concept. Use more than one section when a concept crosses domains.

## Technology and systems

- Prefer standards, official documentation, original papers, and authoritative project documentation.
- Verify whether a term names a strategy, implementation, primitive, protocol, guarantee, or analogy.
- Show failure behavior, concurrency or consistency boundaries, and version-specific caveats.
- Use executable or protocol-level examples when they clarify causality.

## Finance and economics

- Prefer regulators, central banks, statistical agencies, audited filings, exchange documentation, and original academic research.
- Separate identities and formulas from empirical assumptions. Show units, time horizon, nominal versus real values, and compounding convention.
- Date market data, rates, tax rules, laws, product terms, and forecasts.
- Separate education from individualized financial advice. Name uncertainty, downside, liquidity, fees, incentives, and path dependence.
- Prefer a worked scenario or calculator only when assumptions remain visible and editable.

## Science, health, and psychology

- Prefer systematic reviews, meta-analyses, consensus statements, and original studies when needed.
- Distinguish mechanism, correlation, causal evidence, effect size, population, and individual variation.
- State evidence quality and replication limits. Do not promote a study headline into a universal law.
- For health-related concepts, keep the page educational and identify when professional guidance is appropriate.

## History, policy, and law

- Prefer primary documents, legislation, court or government sources, archives, and reputable institutional histories.
- Separate what happened, why historians or analysts think it happened, and what lesson Aaron is drawing now.
- Date laws, policies, officeholders, borders, and institutional rules.
- Show competing interpretations when the dispute changes the concept's usefulness.

## Philosophy and everyday mental models

- Trace a named idea to the thinker or tradition when attribution matters.
- Separate original doctrine from modern popular shorthand.
- Anchor abstraction in a decision Aaron might actually face.
- Include a counterexample: when does the mental model produce a bad judgment?

## Cross-domain synthesis

When borrowing a concept across domains, label the transfer as an analogy unless the mechanisms are genuinely equivalent. Preserve the source domain's meaning before applying it to work, money, relationships, learning, or life.
