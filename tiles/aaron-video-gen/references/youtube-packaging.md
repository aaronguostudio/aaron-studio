# YouTube Packaging

## Title

- Keep it accurate, specific, and usually under 70 characters.
- Create tension through a real contradiction or consequence.
- Make the opening fulfill the title rather than repeat it.

## Thumbnail

- Generate or design two candidates after the visual treatment is selected.
- Use one focal idea and three to five words at most.
- Test at mobile size.
- Match the video's visual spine without turning it into a generic AI thumbnail.
- Prefer a specific object, person, number, or contradiction over decorative
  atmosphere.
- The selected thumbnail may serve as the cover frame, but the cold open should
  move into its first meaningful visual state quickly.

## Description

Use this order:

1. Two or three opening lines that continue the title's promise.
2. A concise summary.
3. Chapters from the final rendered master.
4. Key takeaways.
5. Research and sources.
6. Blog, website, and newsletter links.
7. Relevant hashtags.

## Chapters

Derive chapter timestamps from the final storyboard and rendered master. Do not
use estimates when custom scene timing or music changed the composition.

## Tags

Use 15-20 focused tags across the primary topic, named frameworks, audience,
and broad category. Do not pad the list with unrelated trends.

## Output

Write `<blog-dir>/youtube-metadata.md`:

```markdown
# YouTube Metadata

## Title
[Title]

## Description
[Hook and summary]

CHAPTERS
0:00 - Opening

KEY TAKEAWAYS
- [Takeaway]

SOURCES
- [Source]

READ THE ARTICLE
[URL]

## Tags
[comma-separated tags]
```
