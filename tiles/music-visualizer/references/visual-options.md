# Visual options for the existing Paper Moon track

These are three original visual systems for the same piano track. They share
the same quiet editorial chrome, but they do not share the old moon/orbit
metaphor.

## Ink Current

One continuous asymmetric line carries seven small nodes. The circles are
physically attached to the line and move along it slowly, while a single pulse
travels through the route. This is the strongest option if we want the music to
feel like a current, phrase, or hand-drawn score.

## Paper Resonance

A restrained field of vertical bars rises from one curved baseline. The bars
breathe with the track's chapter energy, and a fine sweep line marks the
current position. This is the clearest and most ownable system for a future
series because it can adapt to piano, ambient, and light electronic tracks.

## Soft Relic

A single layered, asymmetrical form slowly turns and breathes. Offset plates,
one internal seam, and a moving highlight create a quiet 2.5D object without
using a literal orbit or a copied interface language. This is the most
signature option, but it should be used sparingly across a long playlist.

The preview command uses the existing `music.mp3`; it does not call Eleven
Music again.

## Audio mix

`audio-mix` is the combined version. It keeps the title and paper system
stable, then crossfades between the three visual families at analysis-derived
musical boundaries. The individual shapes also respond to smoothed RMS,
low/mid/high-band energy, spectral centroid, and onset pulse. It is a musical
interpretation, not a frame-by-frame equalizer.
