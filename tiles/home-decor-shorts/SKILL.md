## 🎨 Visual Style DNA (from 17 小红书 Reference Photos)

All videos MUST match this specific aesthetic. This is THE look:

### Core Style: Modern European Farmhouse × Quiet Luxury
A blend of:
- **Old World European charm** — arched windows, plaster walls, wainscoting, marble fireplace
- **Modern farmhouse warmth** — shaker cabinets, exposed wood beams, iron fixtures
- **California organic contemporary** — curved furniture, natural materials, indoor-outdoor flow

### Color Palette (STRICT)
- **Walls/ceiling:** Warm white, cream, ivory — NEVER stark/cool white
- **Wood tones:** Honey oak, light pine, warm walnut — visible grain, natural knots
- **Flooring:** Light wide-plank hardwood OR warm limestone/natural stone
- **Metal accents:** Brushed brass, antique gold, oil-rubbed bronze, matte black iron
- **Textile accent:** Navy blue (throw pillows, occasional rug) — the ONE bold color
- **Soft accents:** Sage green, cream, oatmeal, taupe
- **NEVER:** Cool gray, stark white, neon, industrial concrete, dark/moody

### Lighting (Critical — this makes or breaks the video)
- **Golden hour natural light is THE hero** — sunlight streaming through large windows
- Dappled light patterns on hardwood floors from trees outside
- Warm, soft, inviting — NEVER harsh, cool, or artificial-looking
- Dust particles floating in sunbeams
- Supplementary: brass sconces, woven rattan pendants, black iron chandeliers

### Architecture (Key Elements from References)
- **Arched windows/doors** with black steel-framed mullions — signature element
- **French doors** opening to patios/gardens/balconies
- **Exposed wood ceiling beams** (chunky, natural pine/oak)
- **Tongue-and-groove wood plank ceilings** in kitchen zones
- **Wainscoting / wall paneling** with elegant moulding profiles
- **High/vaulted ceilings** — double-height in great rooms
- **White marble fireplace** with classic mantel
- **Built-in bookshelves** in warm oak flanking fireplaces
- **Open floor plans** with room-to-room sightlines through archways

### Furniture (Specific Pieces from References)
- **Cream/ivory bouclé curved sofa** (kidney-shaped) with navy pillows
- **Cream slipcovered deep sofas** — relaxed, lived-in
- **Round/sculptural barrel accent chairs** in cream fabric
- **Natural wood dining tables** — rustic grain, honey-toned, farmhouse scale
- **French-style upholstered bar stools** (curved wood + linen)
- **Mid-century dining chairs** — cream upholstered with slim wood legs
- **Tulip pedestal tables** (Saarinen style) — white, round
- **Bentwood cane-back chairs** (Thonet style) — natural wood + rattan
- **Round wood coffee tables** — chunky pedestal base
- **Rattan/woven baskets**, vintage leather trunks
- **Industrial open shelving** — reclaimed wood + black metal frame

### Decor & Accessories
- **Fresh flowers ALWAYS** — white dahlias, tulips, cherry blossoms, peonies, hydrangeas, magnolia
- **Large glass vases** with olive/eucalyptus branches
- **Curated ceramics** in muted earth tones — terracotta, cream, gray
- **Stacked coffee table books**
- **Vintage Persian or jute rugs** in muted faded tones
- **Linen/sheer curtains** in natural cream — floor length, gently moving
- **Tall indoor olive trees** or fig trees in weathered pots

## 🎬 Camera Movement Rules

**EVERY video MUST have visible, smooth camera movement. NEVER static.**

| Movement | When to Use |
|----------|------------|
| **Dolly forward** — glide into room through doorway/arch | Day 1: reveals, grand entrances |
| **Lateral track** — slide along a scene left-to-right | Day 2: kitchens, long spaces |
| **Push-in** — gentle zoom toward focal point | Day 3: intimate details, desk/nook |
| **Drift** — float from interior toward open doors/windows | Day 4: bedroom → balcony transition |
| **Orbit/arc** — camera arcs around central furniture | Day 5: showcasing room layouts |
| **Walk-through** — continuous from one room to next | Day 6: open floor plans |
| **Pull-back reveal** — start on detail, pull back for full scene | Day 7: detail → room → outdoors |

### Rules:
- **One movement per video** — don't mix
- **Slow and smooth** — luxury = slow. Never fast/jerky
- **Start or end on a detail** — flowers, light on floor, texture close-up
- **Never repeat same movement two days in a row**
