# Prism Breaks — long-form music structure

| Time | Musical role | Production treatment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0:00–0:25 | Arrival | A small electronic pulse settles into view; no hard start. |
| 0:25–2:30 | Settle | Stable 104 BPM broken-beat groove, restrained sub-bass and mallet light. |
| 2:30–4:25 | Variation I | A patient bass/mallet variation changes the colour without changing the focus state. |
| 4:25–5:24 | Opening | A wider cool harmonic layer, still deliberately sparse. |
| 5:24–5:30 | Handoff | Six-second equal-power crossfade between original movements. |
| 5:30–7:30 | Continuation | The groove is already running; no second intro. |
| 7:30–8:45 | Variation II | A filtered answering pluck and darker compatible harmonic shade. |
| 8:45–10:00 | Release | Texture thins into a clean, unresolved close. |

The master uses two separately generated original instrumentals because a
single long Eleven Music request is capped at roughly five and a half minutes.
The two movements are joined with a six-second crossfade rather than repeated
as a loop.
