# Signal Loom — focus direction

## Listening job

Deep work and coding. The image should hold peripheral attention for an hour
without asking the viewer to predict or follow every beat.

## Visual thesis

One quiet signal architecture sits above a deep perspective grid. Six coupled
modules, routed light, and two orbital guides read as one system rather than a
collection of equalizer parts. Geometry follows one deterministic long cycle;
music changes material response only.

## Music mapping

- `calmLow` changes internal green light pressure and glow depth.
- `calmEnergy` changes route visibility and the broad scanning light.
- `calmHigh` changes edge clarity and route-point luminance.
- `pulse` adds one restrained highlight and never changes scale or position.

## Originality boundary

The Endel reference informed restraint, negative space, and the idea of
purpose-specific scenes. Signal Loom's composition, routing language, palette,
motion system, implementation, and audio mapping are original to Aaron Studio.
