## Post copy · English

Most models show one future.

Monte Carlo simulation asks a better question: what range of futures is consistent with our assumptions—and where does the model break?

The useful output is not only the average. It is the distribution, the tail, and the failure paths.

I made an interactive guide where you can change return, volatility, correlation, inflation, fees, and withdrawals, then watch 2,000 possible paths move together.

The important caveat: more simulations reduce sampling noise. They do not rescue weak assumptions.
