# Visual Style Brief: Execution Is No Longer the Bottleneck

## Base language

Soft Glass Narrative, adapted through Editorial Workbench.

## Post-specific style

Sunlit Experiment Workbench.

## Thesis

When execution becomes cheap, the scarce capability is turning ideas into experiments and experiments into systems.

## Reader should feel

Clear, capable, curious, and quietly energized.

## Human-scale anchor

A calm wooden workbench with paper, small physical prototypes, and one finished modular object.

## AI / system layer

A single thin cyan thread quietly links the stages. It implies delegated execution without becoming a glowing interface.

## Palette

Warm white, pale natural wood, graphite, muted stone, soft cyan, and one restrained amber accent.

## Rules

- one left-to-right transformation;
- no more than three stage groups;
- generous wall and tabletop negative space;
- tactile paper, wood, ceramic, and translucent acrylic;
- no text, screens, people, logos, arrows, or fake UI.

## Baseline

Continue the first Builder Note's warm editorial desk world, but change the composition from rest and emptiness to quiet transformation and accumulated learning.
