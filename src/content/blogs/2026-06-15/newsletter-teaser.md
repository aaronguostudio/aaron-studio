Fable 5 did not just feel like a better model. It felt like a different unit of work.

The normal AI loop is prompt-response. I ask, inspect, correct, add context, and ask again. My attention is the control loop.

With Fable, the shape changed. I gave it a multi-hour task, and it planned, delegated, researched, synthesized, and came back with polished pages. The visible output was the page. The more important product was the run.

That is the deeper lesson. When agents can run for hours, the scarce skill is no longer writing the cleverest prompt. It is designing the operating contract around the run: objective, context, authority, checkpoints, evidence, budget, and rollback.
