# Postmortem: FDE Workflow 3 Rewrite

## Prediction

The Workflow 3 edition should outperform the previous draft with qualified technical readers because it changes the article's center of gravity:

- direct opening: four AI platforms converge on the same FDE-style deployment bet;
- original mechanism: a supply-disruption example showing what FDE does from fragmented systems to product write-back;
- personal mechanism: the blog workflow evolves from v1 task chaining to v2 review and learning gates;
- sharper distinction: FDE is not equated with consulting; the article tests whether field work remains connected to production and product;
- practical output: ACTOR maps deployment questions to inspectable artifacts;
- stronger payoff: the FDE label may change, but reliable operating change and recursive learning remain the first principles.

Expected strengths:

- better early relevance because the article names FDE and the current market signal immediately;
- saves and shares around the grounded FDE workflow, customer ownership, and ACTOR;
- strong LinkedIn and technical-leadership fit;
- reusable ACTOR framework for future articles and video.

Expected risks:

- the $7.5B scale signal uses different investment categories and may draw disproportionate debate despite the caveat;
- `human backpropagation` may sound like borrowed Palantir language unless readers notice the broader mechanism and explicit attribution;
- seven sections and roughly 1,700 words should improve mobile completion without stripping the mechanism;
- visual set is intentionally warm-white and editorial; repeated use across future posts could create a new kind of sameness.

## Growth Lesson Applied

`next-brief-context` recommended opening AI-native systems essays with a concrete operating bottleneck. Reader feedback sharpened that lesson: concrete does not mean autobiographical. When the current market event is the reason to read, name the subject first and move the personal operating example to the mechanism it can genuinely support.

Measurement caveat: LinkedIn manual import remains incomplete.

## What Worked Before Publication

- Claim ledger prevented the $7.5B claim from becoming an unqualified headline statistic.
- Editorial contract forced the original contribution into the first 15%.
- Structural redraft cut the old news recap while adding a stronger mechanism and counterargument.
- Reader feedback moved the personal workflow out of the opening, expanded it into a v1-to-v2 learning example, and produced a reusable subject-first hook rule.
- A second feedback pass clarified that connected automation is not the same as a high-quality self-improving system. The durable rule now requires naming the participation burden and the mechanism that converts judgment into reusable system changes.
- A third feedback pass removed the four-output checklist. It competed with ACTOR and added more concepts than the argument needed; one article should have one primary executable framework.
- A fourth feedback pass compressed the draft to seven sections, replaced the three-loop abstraction with a supply-disruption example, folded economics and career implications into their causal sections, and regenerated the two weakest images.
- A fifth feedback pass compressed the investment caveat into one transition, named the blog workflow as a small-scale FDE process, regrouped sentence fragments into coherent paragraphs, and added a non-adversarial consulting/FDE comparison image.
- Concept-first image exploration produced a genuinely different set without deleting earlier styles.
- Semantic visual review rejected a polished ACTOR image with the wrong arrow sequence.
- Package validation caught stale video/script state and verified real image dimensions rather than trusting prompts.

## What Failed Before Publication

- The first four-output image invented several pieces of copy.
- The first ACTOR image encoded the wrong order despite looking polished.
- Requested 2048x1152 images were delivered at 1672x941; sufficient for web, but a reminder that prompt dimensions are not a file guarantee.
- Existing video and YouTube script are now stale relative to the article and must not be treated as current.

## Workflow Lesson

The largest improvement did not come from another prose model. It came from clearer contracts between phases:

```text
claim ledger -> argument -> structural redraft -> scorecard
concept routes -> style decision -> generation manifest -> semantic critique
```

The next workflow should preserve these contracts and resist adding more artifacts unless they close a specific failure mode.

## Next Experiment

For visuals, test one controlled dark or documentary image inside an otherwise light set, then compare reading rhythm rather than choosing by novelty alone.
