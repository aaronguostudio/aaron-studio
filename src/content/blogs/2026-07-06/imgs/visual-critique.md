# Visual Critique: FDE Workflow 3 v8

## Set-Level Review

The six accepted article images plus the thumbnail pass the controlled-mix plan. The cover is loud and tactile; the workflow and closing images are quiet; the FDE mechanism, consulting comparison, and ACTOR framework are medium-weight. The v8 contact sheet confirms visibly different silhouettes across adjacent images.

Continuity comes from warm-white paper, graphite structure, orange input, cyan field signal, green verified result, and restrained print texture. No accepted image uses liquid glass, neon AI imagery, stock meetings, vendor logos, or dark blue/purple gradients.

## Style Fatigue Notes

- No glass or glow.
- Token/input motif appears only in the cover and keyed workflow press, with different silhouettes.
- Only ACTOR uses a five-element framework.
- The FDE mechanism is now a documentary field collage rather than another system diagram.
- The consulting comparison is the only image built around two named professional modes, and it uses a diagonal map-to-engine composition rather than cards.
- The set is still predominantly warm-white; the visual rhythm comes from composition and object scale rather than changing background color. This is intentional, but a future article should test a darker or photographic body image if the overall blog begins to feel too pale.
