# Style Directions: Field Signal Editorial

Concept is locked in `visual-strategy.md`: token input reaches a dead end until a human feedback path closes the operating loop. This file decides how the concept and supporting images are rendered.

## Article Fit Summary

The article is serious but not institutional. It needs strong editorial authority, a human trace, and diagrams that can be reused. The previous all-infographic set was clean, but its repeated white-card grammar flattened the reading rhythm.

## Style Cohesion Decision

`Controlled Mix`, agent-selected under Aaron's delegated decision.

- Primary family: Field Signal Editorial.
- Mechanism variant: Operational Field Collage with the same palette and print treatment.
- Framework variant: Infographic Editorial with exact typography and almost no texture.
- Continuity: warm white paper, graphite black, safety orange, cyan, soft green, muted coral; generous negative space; one predicate per image.

## Why Not Unified

A unified white-card infographic system would repeat the prior set's strongest quality and its main weakness: clarity without pacing. The article now moves through incident, mechanism, economics, risk, career, and framework. Composition should change with those jobs.

## Why Not Experimental Mix

The user delegated the final direction, but this is still a publication set rather than a style laboratory. Three concept routes provide exploration; the accepted set needs a recognizable visual grammar.

## Prompt Implications

- Prompt the primary family as premium editorial print, not retro poster art.
- Keep halftone below 10% of the surface and away from text.
- Use black as structure, orange as token/input, cyan as field signal, green as verified/recursive result, coral as failure.
- Every image must have a distinct silhouette and composition.
- Avoid serif display typography except where ACTOR is intentionally typographic.
