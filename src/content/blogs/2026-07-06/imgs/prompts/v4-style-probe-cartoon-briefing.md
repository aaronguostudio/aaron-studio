# Prompt: v4-style-probe-cartoon-briefing

## Role

Style probe for the FDE article. Test whether a lighter editorial cartoon can make the consulting-vs-FDE distinction memorable.

## Visual Mode

Editorial Minimal

## Visual Weight

medium

## Style Family

Cartoon Briefing

## Positive Prompt

Create a clean editorial cartoon briefing image for an enterprise AI article.

Core visual predicate: the traditional handoff ends at a thick binder, while the forward deployed engineer stays inside the workflow and builds the loop.

Composition:
- 16:9 landscape.
- Split-screen but elegant: left side shows a simplified consultant figure leaving a polished recommendation binder at the edge of a workflow.
- Right side shows a simplified engineer figure standing inside a workflow loop, connecting a model block to data, permissions, users, and outcome measurement.
- Use flat shapes, clean lines, slightly witty editorial tone, and restrained color.
- Make the figures human but generic and minimal; no facial detail.
- Warm paper background, black ink outlines, muted cyan workflow line, amber human-action accent, green review checkpoint.
- It should feel like an intelligent magazine cartoon, not a children's illustration.

Text guidance:
- Avoid text if possible.
- If text appears, only two large labels: HANDOFF and LOOP.

## Negative Prompt

No childish mascots, no slapstick, no exaggerated facial expressions, no dense labels, no comic panels, no speech bubbles, no logos, no fake UI, no messy sketch clutter.

## Mode Constraints

One contrast only: handoff versus embedded building.

## Density Budget

Narrative metaphor with two sides, no more than six major objects.

## Prior Failure To Avoid

Do not compress the entire consulting comparison table into the image.
