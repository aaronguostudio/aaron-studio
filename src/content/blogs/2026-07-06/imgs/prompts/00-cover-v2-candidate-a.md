# Prompt: 00-cover-v2-candidate-a

## Role

Clean blog cover, no text.

## Visual Mode

Editorial Minimal + Real Photo Collage.

## Visual Weight

loud

## Positive Prompt

A high-end editorial business magazine cover image, 16:9. Dark graphite workbench in a quiet enterprise operations room. On the left, a small pile of polished abstract token objects, expensive and inert, catching warm amber light. On the right, a simple paper operating loop made from four large blank cards, a runbook notebook, and one soft green review stamp. A human hand with a pencil draws one clean bridge line from the token pile toward the operating loop. Subtle cyan thread in the background suggests AI intelligence connecting to workflow. Premium realistic paper texture, restrained liquid-glass accent on one card only, generous negative space, crisp composition, warm white and graphite palette, amber and soft green accents. The image should imply: expensive tokens do not become business value until connected to deployment.

## Negative Prompt

No text, no logos, no company marks, no dashboards, no charts, no robots, no brain icon, no cloud icon, no sci-fi HUD, no city skyline, no dense workflow map, no tiny labels, no fake UI, no clutter, no generic stock meeting.

## Mode Constraints

One visual predicate only: tokens connect to a deployment loop. Keep element count low and make the workbench the deployment layer.

## Density Budget

Editorial cover, not a diagram.

## Prior Failure To Avoid

Avoid the previous FDE images' over-explanation through roads, maps, gates, and many small artifacts.
