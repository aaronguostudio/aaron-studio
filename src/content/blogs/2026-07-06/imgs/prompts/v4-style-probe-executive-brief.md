# Prompt: v4-style-probe-executive-brief

## Role

Style probe for the FDE article. Test whether the thesis can work as an authoritative editorial brief visual.

## Visual Mode

Editorial Minimal

## Visual Weight

medium-loud

## Style Family

Executive Brief

## Positive Prompt

Create a high-end executive briefing visual for an article about why AI companies are building deployment organizations.

Core visual predicate: model access is not enough; value appears when deployment capability connects model, workflow, trust, and outcome.

Composition:
- 16:9 landscape.
- A clean board memo / strategy brief surface, seen from above at a slight angle.
- One central decision memo card with four large evidence blocks around it.
- The four blocks are represented as simple physical or graphic modules: MODEL, WORKFLOW, TRUST, OUTCOME.
- A thin amber line moves from model to outcome through the other blocks.
- Use crisp grid alignment, generous margins, premium paper texture, graphite ink, cyan and amber accents.
- It should feel like The Economist / executive strategy memo meets modern product design.

Text guidance:
- Minimal large labels only: MODEL, WORKFLOW, TRUST, OUTCOME.
- No paragraphs, no tiny bullet points.

## Negative Prompt

No generic PowerPoint template, no bullet-heavy slide, no fake financial chart, no bar chart wall, no logos, no stock meeting, no glossy boardroom, no neon AI.

## Mode Constraints

One thesis only: deployment capability connects the parts that turn AI into value.

## Density Budget

Editorial diagram, four blocks maximum plus one central memo.

## Prior Failure To Avoid

Avoid the heavy luxury desk feeling. Make it authoritative but light.
