# Prompt: v8-02-fde-field-loop-candidate-a

## Role

FDE field-work mechanism

## Visual Mode

Human-Scale Metaphor

## Visual Weight

medium

## Style Family

Field Signal Editorial / tactile decision workbench

## Positive Prompt

Use case: stylized-concept. Asset type: 16:9 blog body illustration. One visual predicate: an embedded engineer converts fragmented factory reality into one governed operating decision, then turns the field lesson into a reusable product improvement. On a warm-white editorial paper field, show three distinct operational artifacts entering from the left: a supplier ledger, a factory production sheet, and a customer-order stack, each visually distinct but containing no readable text. At center, realistic human hands actively align those fragments on a physical planning table and pass one decision card through a graphite permission-and-approval gate. At right, one verified green rerouting action enters a real factory workflow represented by a clean conveyor and shipment route. A thin cyan-to-green feedback strip carries one observed exception from the action back to a reusable metal product stencil near the top edge. Premium Field Signal Editorial, crisp Swiss hierarchy, paper cut geometry, graphite linework, safety orange incident accent, signal cyan evidence, verified green action, subtle halftone only in small areas, generous negative space.

## Negative Prompt

No text, labels, logos, dashboards, fake UI, charts, neural networks, brains, robots, glowing AI, glass, neon, purple, circular-arrow infographic, tiny icons, clutter, grunge, watermark.

## Mode Constraints

The hands must be anatomically plausible. One approval gate, one final action, one subtle feedback strip. The image should feel like real operational work rather than an abstract three-box diagram.

## Density Budget

Balanced narrative mechanism.

## Prior Failure To Avoid
