# Content Plan: Expensive Tokens Won't Save Enterprise AI

## Voice Check

**Positioning:** Ship with AI, not about AI. This should read as a builder/operator interpreting a market pattern, not as a finance analyst or AI news commentator.

**Voice rule:** Use first person only where it strengthens the lived operator lens. The piece should not become memoir.

**Personal anchor:** Aaron's own AI workflows across coding, writing, analytics, video, and publishing show the same pattern at small scale: the model is rarely the final bottleneck; the hard part is the system around it.

## Hook / Opening

**Blog hook:** Start with the concrete pattern: Anthropic, OpenAI, AWS, and Microsoft all made moves toward embedded enterprise AI deployment within a short window. Then name the signal: the bottleneck has moved from model access to deployment capability.

**X hook:** Expensive tokens won't save enterprise AI. Anthropic, OpenAI, AWS, and Microsoft are making the same bet: the bottleneck moved from model access to deployment capability.

## Core Argument / Thesis

As frontier models become more powerful and more available, the scarce enterprise AI capability is not access to intelligence. It is deployment: embedding that intelligence into real workflows with context, trust boundaries, measurable outcomes, ownership, and a learning loop.

## Outline

### Section 1: The Pattern Is Too Loud To Ignore
- Four announcements are related, not isolated.
- Anthropic, OpenAI, AWS, and Microsoft are all moving closer to customer workflows.
- The interesting signal is organizational: resources are moving toward deployment capability.

### Section 2: The Demo-To-Deployment Gap
- Enterprise AI stalls between promising demos and reliable production workflows.
- The hard parts are permissions, data boundaries, compliance, existing systems, human behavior, ROI, and ownership.
- Personal anchor: small AI workflows have the same shape; the value emerges only when context, review, and memory are designed.

### Section 3: FDE Is A Lens, Not Just A Job Title
- Define FDE clearly.
- Use Palantir as historical context.
- Distinguish from software engineer, sales engineer, and consultant without caricaturing those roles.

### Section 4: Why The AI Platforms Want This Layer
- Model differentiation alone is not enough.
- Deployment gives platforms customer learning, usage, patterns, stickiness, and access to transformation budgets.
- Karp's critique adds pressure: tokens without workflow change do not become business value.

### Section 5: The Practitioner Lesson
- The durable skill is not prompt engineering; it is workflow engineering.
- Introduce deployment-native engineering.
- Present ACTOR: Action, Context, Trust, Outcome, Recursive ownership.

### Section 6: The Close
- The model era made intelligence abundant.
- The deployment era will decide who can turn intelligence into work.
- Final critique: companies that cannot answer the deployment question will keep buying expensive tokens and reporting activity as progress.
