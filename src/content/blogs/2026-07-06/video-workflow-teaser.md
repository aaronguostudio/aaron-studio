# Video Workflow Upgrade Teaser

## Core Angle

I spent the weekend stress-testing GPT-5.6S to improve the video workflow behind the FDE essay. The important result is not that a newer model generated prettier scenes. The quality jump came from giving the model a directed workflow: story beats, layout constraints, pacing, music, voice, and frame-level review.

Use the new FDE video as proof. Do not frame the post as a generic model review or imply that the model made the film on its own.

## Recommended Asset

Use a 20-30 second native excerpt from the FDE video that shows the strongest visual transition and your new voice. It should make sense with sound off, but keep the music and narration on for people who choose to listen.

## LinkedIn

**Format:** Native 20-30 second video excerpt. Put the article link in the first comment, not in the post body.

The first version of my FDE video worked. It still looked like AI made it.

This weekend, I hit the GPT-5.6S usage limit twice while rebuilding it. I was trying to make one seven-minute video feel like a finished film, not a slide deck with motion.

The old workflow could turn a blog post into a voice track, visuals, and animation. But it still had the usual AI-video problems: abrupt scene changes, layouts that drifted as they animated, and no real visual rhythm.

The breakthrough was not asking for more effects. It was treating the process like direction:

- story beats before scenes
- a small layout system instead of a new composition every time
- music, voice, and motion designed together
- frame-level QA when animation changes the layout

GPT-5.6S made the iteration loop much faster. But the quality leap came from the workflow around the model: constraints, taste, and review.

The result is the new FDE video I published yesterday.

What part of your AI workflow still works, but does not yet feel finished?
