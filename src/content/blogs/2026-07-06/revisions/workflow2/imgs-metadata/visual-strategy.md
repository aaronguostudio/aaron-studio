# Visual Strategy

## Article Character

Enterprise / market argument with a technical framework section. The article is not a generic AI news recap; it argues that enterprise AI value has moved from model access to deployment capability, then gives technical people the ACTOR framework.

## Reader Emotion

The reader should feel a clean shift in perspective: first a little tension about expensive token spend, then relief that the deployment problem can be made concrete, and finally a practical sense of "I can use this lens in my own work."

## Visual Risk

- Repeating the previous FDE set's over-explanation problem.
- Turning every image into a workflow map.
- Making AI look like generic glowing dashboards or sci-fi infrastructure.
- Letting the ACTOR framework become five small illustrated cards instead of one memorable operator diagram.
- Using too much glass, route-line glow, or hand-drawn texture across the whole set.

## Recommended Mode Mix

- Cover and thumbnail: Editorial Minimal with a grounded Real Photo Collage feeling.
- Body 01: Editorial Minimal, focused on convergence into deployment rather than a literal landscape.
- Body 02: Human-Scale Metaphor, quieter and more concrete than the old glowing loop.
- Body 03: Operator Diagram, clean comparison with one visual predicate.
- Body 04: Operator Diagram with a light Glass / Fluent Artifact accent for ACTOR.

## Cover Direction

Show the conflict in the title without using text: expensive polished tokens sitting visibly apart from a real operating loop on a workbench. A human hand or pencil bridges the two. The cover should feel like a high-end business magazine image: restrained, sharp, slightly dramatic, and not "AI art."

## Body Image Direction

Body images should give the reader rhythm. The first can be a stronger thesis visual, but the middle image should be calmer and human-scale. Avoid making all body images diagrams. Each image should carry one visual predicate and leave enough negative space to read comfortably in the article.

## Framework / Diagram Direction

The comparison diagram and ACTOR diagram should use large shapes, readable hierarchy, and very few internal details. The ACTOR image may use the letters A C T O R, but each tile should stay clean. The important visual idea is the recursive path from R back to A.

## What To Avoid

- No logos for Anthropic, OpenAI, AWS, Microsoft, Palantir, or consulting firms.
- No dense road maps, city skylines, dashboard walls, tiny labels, or fake UI.
- No more than one glowing route-line image in the final set.
- No internal icon clusters inside the ACTOR tiles.
- No stock-photo boardroom, smiling corporate people, or abstract robot/brain/cloud imagery.
