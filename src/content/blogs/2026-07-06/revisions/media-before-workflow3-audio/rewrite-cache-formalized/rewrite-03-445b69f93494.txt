I encounter a smaller version of this in my own publishing workflow. 

When someone claims AI can produce a solid article in five minutes, they’re overlooking a significant amount of effort involved.

---SEGMENT---

My first workflow brought together research, outlining, writing, translation, images, audio, video, and publishing. It was quick and efficient. However, it also had a tendency to carry weak evidence, awkward language, repetitive visuals, and broken links right through to the final product.

---SEGMENT---

The second version demanded more engagement. I needed to reread, reject, and challenge claims. I compared visual directions and tested the final package. Quality improved as those judgments turned into evidence gates, editorial rules, and validators.

While this doesn’t confirm that FDE operates effectively at the enterprise level, it does illustrate a similar working pattern: staying close to actual work, identifying gaps in general capability, and integrating corrections back into the system.
