# Guizang x Remotion FDE Study

## Intent

This is a visual study, not a replacement for the published FDE film. It tests a
useful division of labor: Guizang supplies the art direction and constrained
layout vocabulary; Remotion supplies the timed, inspectable motion.

## The Experiment

The study uses three editorial layout families from `layout-manifest.json`:

1. `cover-editorial` establishes the existing article identity at frame zero.
2. `convergence-ledger` turns the four-company signal into a disciplined evidence page rather than a dark dashboard.
3. `operating-loop` explains the model-to-field-to-product feedback loop with nodes that establish before their connectors animate.

## What Is Deliberately Excluded

- No raw slide capture or slide-to-video pan.
- No generic black-and-green control-room styling.
- No free-form per-scene layout decisions.
- No 3D motion unless a later director plan gives it a narrative job.

## Review Questions

- Does the warm editorial identity feel closer to Aaron's publishing brand?
- Does the named layout system make the scene hierarchy clearer at a glance?
- Does constrained motion preserve the design after animation begins?
- Which layout families are valuable enough to promote into the production scene registry?
