# FDE Full Film Treatment

## Product Promise

Turn the complete article into a seven-and-a-half-minute editorial film that
explains why FDE matters, shows what the work looks like, and leaves the viewer
with ACTOR as a practical deployment framework.

## Visual Spine

Use `signal-editorial-v0.1`: a near-black field, paper only for model or document
surfaces, Signal jade for learning and retained capability, and coral only for
gaps, handoffs, lock-in, or unresolved risk. The same header, grid, captions,
type hierarchy, flow nodes, and result bands should survive every chapter.

## Scene Mix

- Evidence: company convergence, scale signal, fragmented factory data.
- Explanation: operating layer, governed decision, field-to-product learning,
  workflow gates, consulting/FDE comparison, and customer-owned assets.
- Emphasis: five-minute myth, ACTOR framework, token-versus-value ending.
- Signature motion: one bounded 3D model turn. All other scenes remain flat or
  lightly dimensional and prioritize reading.

## Motion Budget

Each narration section contains two or three mutually exclusive visual stages.
Scaffolds may remain visible between stages, but large statements never sit on
top of dense evidence. Information-bearing 3D planes use the approved transform
envelope: 36-degree maximum Y rotation, 80-percent minimum projected width, and
64px clearance from protected zones.

## Music

Keep the approved 80-second cello opening from the V3 prototype. Crossfade into
a single long-form continuation before the factory chapter. The continuation
uses the same low cello, restrained piano, and analog pulse; it lifts only for
ACTOR and resolves under the final customer-ownership statement.

## Prototype Contract

The V3 82-second prototype is the approved baseline for company convergence,
the deployment reveal, the consulting comparison, ACTOR, captions, voice, and
music direction. New full-film scenes must pass entry, peak, exit, and encoded
contact-sheet checks before the master is delivered.
