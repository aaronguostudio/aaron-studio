# Blog Plan: Expensive Tokens Won't Save Enterprise AI

## Meta

- **Author:** Aaron Guo
- **Target:** aaronguo.com, English and Simplified Chinese
- **Tone:** Entrepreneurial, crisp, evidence-led, technically serious; no vendor cheerleading or consultant caricature
- **Length:** 1,500-1,900 English words
- **Audience:** Senior engineers, technical/product leaders, and operators responsible for enterprise AI
- **CTA:** Leave the reader with a diagnostic question, not a newsletter pitch

## Hook

Open directly on FDE. Anthropic, OpenAI, AWS, and Microsoft are all moving engineers closer to customers, and three disclosed at least $7.5B of commitments within 59 days. The point is not the money. It is what the convergence says about where the model product still ends.

## Thesis

FDE is becoming important because model capability does not become operating change by itself. FDEs connect real decisions, fragmented systems, permissions, and field feedback, while the customer must retain ownership of the resulting workflow.

## Personal Anchor

Use the five-minute article myth and Aaron's blog workflow evolution from v1 to v2 after the human-backpropagation mechanism is established. Connect it to the one-person-project ownership model, but state explicitly that it illustrates feedback topology rather than proving enterprise impact.

## Story Movement

### 1. $7.5B says the bottleneck is deployment

- Compress Anthropic, OpenAI, AWS, and Microsoft into one pattern, supported by primary links.
- Clarify that vendor announcements prove priorities, not outcomes.
- Fold in why model companies are hiring people: delivery plus field-to-product learning.

### 2. What FDE actually does in the field

- Use Palantir's official methodology and supply-chain nouns/verbs.
- Walk through a manufacturer facing a supply disruption.
- Show the work: connect systems, model decisions and actions, add permissions and approvals, observe exceptions, return reusable failures to product.
- Fold the career signal into this section.

### 3. Do not be fooled by five-minute AI

- Distinguish generation, production, and self-improvement.
- Keep the v1-to-v2 example short and emphasize participation and maintenance.

### 4. Do not equate FDE with consulting

- Acknowledge consulting and SI capabilities without caricature.
- Keep one distinction: field work remains connected to production and core product.

### 5. The workflow must belong to the customer

- Make the lock-in position explicit.
- Name the workflow assets the customer must own in prose, not a second framework.
- End on capability transfer rather than rented outcomes.

### 6. ACTOR is Aaron's deployment framework

- Keep the five questions concise.
- Apply each step to this article's actual production workflow.
- Use the removed four-output checklist as the real `Recursive` example.

### 7. Tokens are inputs; changed work is value

- Give one portable operating rule.
- End on customer-owned operating systems, not the longevity of the FDE title.

## Evidence Placement

- Put company links in the market-pattern section.
- Put Palantir link at the human-backpropagation definition.
- Use Palantir's architecture docs for the concrete supply-chain example.
- Avoid a bibliography section; keep citations beside supported claims.

## Visual Ideas

- Cover: a polished token receipt or meter feeding no movement into a real workflow, with an embedded engineer creating the missing feedback path.
- FDE field work: one supply disruption moving from fragmented systems to a governed decision, with field learning returning to product.
- ACTOR: five large typographic steps with `R` visibly returning to `A`.
- Closing: expensive token input enters a workflow the customer owns; verified value and reusable learning leave it.
