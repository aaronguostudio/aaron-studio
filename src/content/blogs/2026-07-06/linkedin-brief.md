Anthropic, OpenAI, AWS, and Microsoft are all moving in the same direction: embedding engineers inside customer organizations to make AI actually work.

I do not think this is a coincidence.

It tells us where the enterprise AI bottleneck moved. The hard part is no longer only model capability. It is deployment: connecting AI to real workflows, data permissions, governance, user habits, fallback paths, and measurable business outcomes.

That is why forward deployed engineering is suddenly becoming mainstream again. It is not just consulting with a cooler title. At its best, it is engineering, product judgment, implementation, and operating change compressed into one loop.

For technical people, the signal is clear: the durable skill is not prompt engineering. It is workflow engineering.

The useful lens is ACTOR: Action, Context, Trust, Outcome, Responsibility. The last layer is where the feedback loop lives: who owns learning after launch?
