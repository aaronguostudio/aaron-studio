# Video Treatment: FDE Field Signal Prototype

## Product Promise

Make the viewer feel the enterprise deployment gap before naming it, then leave
them with ACTOR as a compact way to inspect whether AI has changed real work.
The prototype should feel authored, restrained, and complete enough to enjoy as
a short film rather than a technical animation test.

## Reference Deconstruction

### Reference A: William Candillon, The Shader's Gambit

- URL: https://x.com/wcandillon/status/2015345960491069718
- Sound before first image: a low classical phrase establishes tension before
  the visual argument becomes explicit.
- First-ten-second contract: music, spatial depth, and a decisive scene change
  promise a designed experience immediately.
- Camera and spatial behavior: planes turn in perspective; movement reveals
  structure rather than decorating a static layout.
- Typography and layout: large editorial statements alternate with visual
  systems instead of remaining on one slide grid.
- Pacing and intensity curve: a composed opening, denser explanatory middle,
  and clean resolution.
- Borrow: sound-led entry, one 3D reveal, authored transitions, confident holds.
- Avoid: shader spectacle without explanatory value, constant camera motion,
  and effects that make text harder to read.

### Reference B: Motion and React Bits examples

- URLs: https://motion.dev/docs/react-animate-number and
  https://github.com/DavidHDev/react-bits
- Borrow: staggered builds, number emphasis, mask reveals, and purposeful
  micro-motion as a vocabulary for selected beats.
- Avoid: copying interactive components directly into Remotion, random
  animation presets, and animating every element merely because it can move.

## Recommended Directions

### Direction A: Field Signal Editorial

- Visual spine: near-black field, warm paper panels, signal green, oxidized red,
  fine grid lines, and one continuous route that changes meaning by scene.
- Scene families: evidence convergence, spatial system reveal, split comparison,
  progressive framework.
- Signature opportunity: a model card rotates in 3D and reveals the operational
  layers hidden behind a polished API surface.
- Tradeoff: needs one custom composition, but the visual grammar can become a
  reusable template family.

### Direction B: Executive Briefing in Motion

- Visual spine: white editorial canvas, strict Swiss grid, black typography,
  and a restrained orange annotation layer.
- Scene families: source cards, comparison table, process diagram, framework
  cards.
- Signature opportunity: the consulting handoff line transforms into a closed
  FDE learning loop.
- Tradeoff: faster and highly legible, but less atmospheric and less distinct
  from a polished presentation.

## Selected Direction

- Selection: Direction A with Direction B's disciplined information hierarchy.
- Why: Aaron explicitly responded to William's sound, spatial turns, and product
  quality, while asking that motion remain selective and template-constrained.
- Aaron approval: confirmed by continuing with the proposed 60-90 second FDE
  prototype after the Video Workflow 2.0 upgrade.

## Scene Mix

- Evidence: four company signals converge into one deployment move.
- Explanation: model surface opens into data, permissions, approvals, exceptions,
  and recovery.
- Explanation: consulting handoff versus the FDE field-to-product loop.
- Emphasis: ACTOR builds progressively and resolves into customer capability.
- Calm / structured / signature balance: 25 / 55 / 20.

## Motion Language

- Primary recipes: stagger, connector-draw, focus-shift, card-build, mask-reveal.
- Experimental recipe: one frame-driven 3D card turn.
- Effects explicitly excluded: continuous parallax, ambient particles, bouncing
  text, decorative code rain, and repeated full-screen image flips.

## Music And Sound

- Strategy: continuous, with a two-second music-led opening and restrained
  narration ducking.
- Opening cue: low cello pulse with space between notes.
- Section cues: subtle pulse enters at the deployment reveal; one muted impact
  marks the comparison; a small harmonic lift begins ACTOR.
- Ending cue: clean held resolution under the ownership statement.
- Music source and rights: original instrumental generated for this prototype;
  record provider, prompt, model, and request metadata beside the asset.
- Sound effects: one soft paper turn and one low loop closure, only if the music
  leaves enough space.

## Prototype Slice

- Time range: approximately 0:00-1:20.
- Calm scene: company convergence and opening question.
- Structured scene: consulting versus FDE.
- Signature scene: 3D deployment reveal followed by the ACTOR build.
- Questions the prototype must answer: Does music add authority without masking
  Aaron's voice? Does the 3D turn clarify the argument? Can ACTOR build quickly
  without recreating the blank-screen problem? Does the mixed scene language
  still feel like one product?
