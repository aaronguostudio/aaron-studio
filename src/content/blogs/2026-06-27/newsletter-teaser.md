I am starting a new column called Curiosity Notes.

The idea is simple: start with one fact that refuses to stay small, then follow it until it becomes a deeper lesson about systems, judgment, history, or human behavior.

The first piece starts with a strange spaceflight detail: before Artemis II broke the record in April 2026, the farthest humans had ever been from Earth was Apollo 13.

Apollo 13 never landed on the Moon. It became an emergency return.

That means humanity's farthest old record came from a failed mission. The essay is about what that says about progress, resilience, and the difference between a system's aspiration frontier and its recovery frontier.
