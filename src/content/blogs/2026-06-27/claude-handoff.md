## Topic

Working title:

**The Farthest Humans Ever Went Started as a Failure**

Chinese title:

**人类最远的距离，曾经来自一次失败**

Core fact:

In April 2026, Artemis II surpassed Apollo 13's human spaceflight distance record. Apollo 13 had held the old record for 56 years, even though it was a failed lunar landing mission that became an emergency return.

This creates the central tension:

> For more than half a century, the farthest humans had ever been from Earth came from a mission that failed to achieve its original goal.

## Core Sources

Use these as factual anchors:

- NASA Artemis II mission recap: https://www.nasa.gov/centers-and-facilities/johnson/artemis-ii-mission-milestones-an-image-and-video-recap/
- NASA Apollo 13 mission page: https://www.nasa.gov/mission_pages/apollo/missions/apollo13.html

Relevant facts to preserve:

- Artemis II reached about **252,756 miles** from Earth.
- Apollo 13 reached about **248,655 miles** from Earth.
- Apollo 13 did not land on the Moon because an oxygen tank explosion turned the mission into an emergency return.
- Artemis II was a planned crewed lunar flyby and systems test.

## What the Current Draft Gets Right

## Suggested Direction

One possible stronger thesis:

> The most interesting thing about Apollo 13 is not that failure led to success. It is that a failed mission became the historical measurement of how far a human system could travel away from certainty and still return.

Possible structure:

1. Open with the record and the contradiction.
2. Reconstruct why Apollo 13's distance is emotionally different from Artemis II's.
3. Explore the idea that "farthest" is not only a physical measure.
4. Show why failure reveals the hidden radius of a system.
5. Bring in the operator lens only after the essay has earned it.
6. End with the distinction between:
   - the distance of aspiration;
   - the distance of return.
