## Description

AI coding does not just make developers faster. It changes where software teams slow down.

In this video, I explain why the old delivery rhythm of BA, design, development, QA, UAT, and deploy starts to break when one person can use AI to clarify requirements, compare options, write code, generate tests, draft docs, and summarize risk in one working session.

The answer is not to remove QA or weaken teamwork. The answer is to redesign collaboration around one accountable owner, useful AI agents, shared team boundaries, and evidence that makes speed trustworthy.

Core idea:
One-person project does not mean solo work. It means single ownership inside a shared alignment system.

## Pinned Comment Draft

The phrase "one-person project" is easy to misunderstand. I do not mean one person working alone. I mean one accountable owner using AI agents inside clear team boundaries, with enough evidence for the team to trust the change.

The four questions I would start with:

Who owns this end to end?
Which agents can they use?
Where are the team boundaries?
What evidence would make us trust this enough to ship?
