## Hook / Opening

**Blog hook:** On one screen, ChatGPT Work spent about 15 minutes producing a sourced 10-slide PowerPoint. On another, Claude Cowork turned more than 100 receipts into an 84-line expense workbook and flagged the rows it could not trust. The interesting difference is not which model sounded smarter. It is what kind of workbench each product is becoming.

**X hook:** ChatGPT Work has more surface area. Claude Cowork has more scar tissue. For finance, the winner isn't the agent with the best demo - it's the workbench that can preserve evidence, editability, approval, and recovery through the full run.

## Core Argument / Thesis

ChatGPT Work and Claude Cowork are competing to own four planes of knowledge work: context, execution, artifact, and control. ChatGPT Work currently integrates more surfaces; Claude Cowork currently has stronger evidence for file-native, plugin-driven Office work. Finance teams should choose by workflow architecture and run-level proof, not model reputation or launch-day feature count.

## Outline

### 1. Two Outputs, Not Two Press Releases

- Contrast the visible ChatGPT deck and Cowork expense workbook.
- State the asymmetric evidence window immediately.
- Earn the thesis in the first 150 words: this is a control-plane comparison.

### 2. The Products Have Converged Faster Than the Narratives

- Compact feature matrix: cloud/desktop, local files, browser/computer use, plugins, schedules, artifacts, finance integration, governance.
- ChatGPT Work: Chat/Work/Codex, built-in browser, Sites, unified plugin surface.
- Cowork: file-native execution, plugin/skills ecosystem, Office workflow depth, web/mobile cloud.
- Do not announce a permanent winner.

### 3. Surface Area vs Scar Tissue

- ChatGPT launch evidence: sourced `.pptx`, Sites/dashboard, cross-app ambition.
- Cowork mature evidence: receipts to workbook, editable PowerPoint reconstruction, scheduled tasks.
- Mature pain: usage ceilings, history/memory incident, externalized memory and backup.
- Mechanism: a workbench becomes valuable and risky when it owns state across runs.

### 4. Why Finance Changes the Comparison

- Finance work is a chain: source data → transformation → model → commentary/deck → approval.
- A plausible answer is insufficient; the artifact must remain editable, linked, reviewable, and recoverable.
- Connect to Aaron's prior finance test: structured starting point mattered more than perfect output.
- Explain separate products: ChatGPT for Excel and Claude for Excel/PowerPoint complement the broader work agents.

### 5. The Four-Plane Test

1. Context: where evidence and institutional knowledge live.
2. Execution: cloud/local, tools, schedules, computer use.
3. Artifact: formulas, linked cells, editable slides, source references, exceptions.
4. Control: permissions, approvals, lineage, recovery, ownership.

- Apply each plane to the two products in a concise table.
- Provide a same-task finance pilot: reconcile a mock month-end package, update a model, build a variance deck, and produce an exception log.

### 6. The Honest Answer May Be a Boundary, Not a Winner

- Counterargument: use both.
- Personal multi-tool stacks are easy; institutional stacks need explicit data and responsibility boundaries.
- Choose Cowork first when local files, customizable domain plugins, and Office artifact depth dominate.
- Choose ChatGPT Work first when work spans SaaS tools, research, Sites, and a unified Chat/Codex surface.
- Keep evaluations, permissions, audit history, and runbooks portable.

### 7. Conclusion

- Reframe: the durable asset is not a vendor preference but the organization's own workflow control layer.
- The product that wins finance is the one that can carry evidence into an editable deliverable without losing the human review boundary.
