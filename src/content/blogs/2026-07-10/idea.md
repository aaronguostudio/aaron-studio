# Idea

## Topic

Compare OpenAI's newly launched ChatGPT Work with Anthropic's Claude Cowork, then translate the feature race into a decision for finance teams.

## Why Now

OpenAI launched ChatGPT Work on July 9, 2026, two days after Anthropic expanded Claude Cowork to web and mobile. The two products now compete for the same layer of knowledge work: connected context, long-running execution, editable deliverables, recurring tasks, and review.

## Target Reader

A product, technology, or finance leader deciding how agentic work should enter a regulated knowledge-work environment.

## Reader Pain / Curiosity

Launch coverage lists features but does not answer the operational question: which product is ready for finance work, what evidence exists beyond vendor demos, and what should a team test before standardizing?

## Initial Thesis

ChatGPT Work and Claude Cowork are not primarily competing on model intelligence. They are competing to become the control plane around knowledge work. ChatGPT Work enters with a broader integrated surface; Claude Cowork enters the comparison with a more mature file, plugin, and Office-deliverable workflow. For finance, the winner is the system that can preserve evidence, permissions, editability, review, and recovery across the full workflow.

## Why This Should Exist

Aaron has already tested Claude's finance plugins on real DCF, reporting, and recommendation workflows. This article upgrades that earlier conclusion from "skills are becoming the product" to "the workspace around those skills is becoming the product."

## Kill Criteria

- Kill or narrow the article if ChatGPT Work is treated as proven on long-duration finance work after only one launch day.
- Do not publish a generic feature checklist without an operator decision lens.
- Do not use community claims that cannot be traced to a specific task, artifact, failure, or source.
