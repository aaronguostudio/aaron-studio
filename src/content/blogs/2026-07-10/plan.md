# Blog Plan: ChatGPT Work vs Claude Cowork: Finance Doesn't Need a Smarter Chatbot

## Meta

- **Author:** Aaron Guo
- **Target:** aaronguo.com blog (EN + ZH bilingual)
- **Tone:** Entrepreneurial, crisp, evidence-led, commercially useful; no generic AI-review voice or replacement hype
- **Length:** 2,200-2,800 English words
- **Audience:** Product, technology, and finance leaders evaluating an agentic workspace
- **CTA:** Follow

## Hook

Open on two screens and two deliverables. A launch-day ChatGPT Work walkthrough shows a sourced 10-slide `.pptx` after roughly 15 minutes. A Cowork tutorial recommended by its own community shows more than 100 receipts becoming an 84-line `.xlsx` with formulas, currency summaries, and uncertain rows marked for verification. The visible difference is not model eloquence. It is the workbench around the model.

Within 150 words, state the caveat and thesis: ChatGPT Work has one day of public evidence; Cowork has six months. The comparison is not a permanent winner. It is about which product is closer to owning the context, execution, artifact, and control planes of knowledge work.

## Thesis

ChatGPT Work currently has the broader integrated surface, Claude Cowork currently has the stronger public evidence for file-native and editable Office work, but finance teams should choose by which system preserves evidence and control across the full run rather than by model reputation or launch-day feature count.

## Personal Anchor

In February, Aaron inspected Anthropic's finance plugin repository and ran DCF, client-report, and investment-recommendation work. The important result was not perfect output; it was turning a blank workbook into a correctly structured, editable starting point. The new products move the question one level up: can the workspace carry that starting point through data, tools, review, and recovery?

## Outline

### Part 1: Two Deliverables, Not Two Press Releases

- Describe the ChatGPT `.pptx` and Cowork receipt workbook with direct source links and screenshot captions.
- Explain why the Cowork example is especially revealing for finance: it flags uncertain rows instead of hiding them.
- State the asymmetric observation period and refuse a permanent winner.
- Land the original judgment: the real contest is the work control plane.

### Part 2: The Products Converged; Their Evidence Did Not

- Use one compact comparison table: surfaces, local/cloud, apps/browser, plugins, schedules, artifacts, finance, maturity.
- ChatGPT Work: Chat/Work/Codex inside one product, plugins, browser, Sites, web/mobile/desktop.
- Cowork: local files, connector-browser-computer-use hierarchy, file-based plugins, Office work, cloud web/mobile.
- Explain the distinction between documented capability and demonstrated operating maturity.

### Part 3: Surface Area vs Scar Tissue

- ChatGPT Work's launch advantage is integrated breadth and the ability to turn analysis into shareable Sites.
- Cowork's current advantage is the density of real file and Office use cases.
- Add the editable PowerPoint reconstruction screenshot as artifact proof.
- Add the real costs of maturity: usage ceilings and the history-loss anecdote; balance the anecdote with commenters who did not lose history.
- Mechanism: once a workspace owns long-running state, memory and recovery become product features.

### Part 4: Finance Makes the Difference Visible

- Use the finance chain: source → reconciliation/transformation → model → commentary/deck → approval.
- Reference Aaron's earlier finance plugin test and the structured-starting-point thesis.
- Compare ecosystems without conflation: ChatGPT for Excel and Claude for Excel/PowerPoint are companion surfaces, not identical to Work/Cowork.
- Explain why answer quality alone fails: finance needs editable formulas, linked cells, source lineage, exception logs, and review.

### Part 5: The Four-Plane Test

- Introduce the only framework in the article:
  - Context plane
  - Execution plane
  - Artifact plane
  - Control plane
- Apply it to each product in a decision table.
- Provide a same-input pilot: a synthetic month-end close package that requires reconciling data, updating a model, writing variance commentary, building a deck, and leaving an exception log.
- Define proof: not speed alone, but source traceability, editable output, surfaced uncertainty, approval, and recovery.

### Part 6: The Honest Answer May Be a Boundary, Not a Winner

- Give clear selection guidance:
  - Cowork-first when local files, organization-specific skills/plugins, and Office artifact fidelity dominate.
  - ChatGPT Work-first when the workflow spans many SaaS apps, web research, shareable Sites, and a unified Chat/Codex surface.
- Address "use both": valid for individuals, incomplete for institutions.
- Require one owner per workflow and explicit boundaries for data, permissions, audit history, and handoff.
- Keep durable assets portable: skills, templates, evals, permission rules, audit history, runbooks.

### Part 7: The Workspace Is Becoming the Product

- Upgrade the February thesis: skills remain important, but the workspace that carries skills through a governed run is now the competitive layer.
- The winning product is not the one that generates the most impressive first answer; it is the one that lets the organization understand, review, recover, and improve the work.
- Finish on the finance test: if the source cannot be traced and the workbook cannot be reviewed, the task is not done.
- CTA: follow for more operator research at the intersection of financial services and AI-native execution.
