# Visual Style Brief v2: I Gave Codex a Task From a Moving Tesla

## Base Language

Soft Glass Narrative

## Post-Specific Style Name

Roadside Soft Glass OS

## Article Thesis

AI's biggest productivity shift is not faster task completion; it is the move from doing tasks manually to designing a human-agent work system that can execute, report back, and learn.

## Reader Should Feel

Calm, curious, modern, human, quietly amazed.

## Human-Scale Anchor

A family road trip, a phone used as a control surface, a desk left behind while work continues, a notebook becoming a route, a room that quietly organizes itself, and a life scene where AI is background infrastructure.

## AI / Glass Layer

The AI operating system should appear as a quiet second layer:

- soft glass ribbons;
- one or two cyan threads;
- a warm amber road/path line;
- a small green review checkpoint;
- translucent sheets that imply memory or workflow;
- objects subtly arranging themselves.

Do not show AI primarily as a dense dashboard, command center, glowing network, or wall of charts.

## Palette

- Ink graphite for contrast and night-road depth.
- Warm white for paper, glass, and calm surfaces.
- Soft cyan for delegated AI traces.
- Warm amber for intent, road, time, and life direction.
- Soft green for review and trust.
- Muted coral only for friction or risk.
- Dusty violet only for creative/video scenes.

The set should feel warmer and lighter than the v1 `Personal AI Operating System Atlas` images.

## Image Mix

Target body set:

- 6 narrative/metaphor images;
- 1 light architecture diagram;
- 1 clean framework diagram;
- 0 dense dashboard images.

Allowed mechanism images:

- Notion as dispatch layer over distributed memory.
- Intent -> skill -> run -> review -> memory.

Everything else should be scene, metaphor, or editorial illustration.

## Recurring Motifs

- Amber road/path line: life moving forward.
- Cyan thread: delegated work continuing quietly.
- Green gate/checkpoint: human review.
- Warm white sheets: memory, notes, docs, artifacts.
- Open window/door: work leaving the desk.
- Soft glass pane: invisible infrastructure.
- A small lamp/light: AI working in the background.

## Image Set Rules

1. Use flat editorial composition with restrained liquid-glass accents.
2. Prefer people, objects, rooms, roads, windows, paper, light, and everyday surfaces over dashboards.
3. Keep the AI layer secondary unless the image is explicitly one of the two mechanism images.
4. Limit most images to one subject, one supporting object, and one AI/glass layer.
5. Avoid fake readable text; if text-like forms appear, make them abstract marks.
6. Keep enough negative space for comfortable blog reading.
7. Preserve a premium, modern feel without using heavy 3D neon effects.

## Anti-Patterns

- Dense chart walls.
- Glowing AI dashboard rooms.
- Sci-fi HUD overlays.
- Generic AI brain, robot, cloud, or network.
- Fake UI labels.
- Full 3D rendered command centers.
- Isometric SaaS explosions.
- Every image becoming a system map.
- Dark-blue-only palette.

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] The image can be described in one sentence.
- [ ] It has a human-scale anchor or strong metaphor.
- [ ] The AI/glass layer is quiet and secondary.
- [ ] It has enough negative space for blog reading.
- [ ] It avoids fake text, chart clutter, and neon HUD energy.
- [ ] It belongs to the same series as the cover and other body images.
- [ ] It makes the article feel easier to read, not more cognitively demanding.
