# 越是 AI 时代，越要仰望星空

## Raw Idea

I want to write about this theme: the more we enter the AI era, the more we need to look up at the stars.

This comes from a work observation: people's results with AI vary wildly. Some people spend a long time and still cannot get the output they want. Some people only need one or two prompts.

Real life is the same. Some salespeople can meet a client once and almost close the deal. Others spend enormous effort and still cannot move the relationship forward.

AI is like a mirror. A person's ability to use AI often reflects their life experience, knowledge, judgment, taste, and breadth of understanding. AI may even widen that gap. It gives leverage, but leverage magnifies what is already there.

So the point is: the more powerful AI becomes, the more important it is to raise ourselves. We need to keep learning, enrich our lives, widen our horizons, and build deeper judgment. In the AI era, we need to look up at the stars.

## Core Claim

AI does not automatically flatten human differences. It reveals and amplifies the differences in people's accumulated knowledge, lived experience, judgment, and imagination.
