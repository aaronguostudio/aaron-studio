# Video Brief

## Target Audience

Technology and space viewers who recognize Starship launches but do not know
which engineering outcomes separate a useful test from another spectacular
clip.

## Desired Emotion

Immediate awe, followed by the satisfaction of knowing exactly what to watch.

## Core Promise

In under one minute, explain why Starship Flight 13 matters through three
specific test objectives, using current official footage and primary sources.

## Title/Thumbnail Expectation

Title: `Starship Flight 13: The Three Tests That Matter`

Thumbnail promise: `NOT JUST THE LAUNCH`

## High-Shock Facts

- SpaceX is targeting July 16, 2026 for Flight 13.
- The FAA closed the Flight 12 mishap investigation on July 13.
- The FAA identified heat effects and incorrect engine-alarm settings as the
  two most probable booster-loss causes.
- SpaceX identified four corrective actions.
- Flight 13 plans a complete booster boostback burn and soft splashdown.
- The flight plans to deploy twenty functional Starlink V3 satellites.
- Starship plans an in-space Raptor relight.
- NASA is working with SpaceX on Starship HLS for Artemis missions.

## Hook Type

Reframe: the spectacular launch is not the most important part of this test.
The meaningful story is whether the repaired system can complete three
specific operations.

## Story Structure

Official rollout -> launch-date promise -> Flight 12 failure -> FAA causes ->
three test objectives -> archived launch image -> lunar-program consequence.

## Retention Beat Map

- 0s: official 4K booster rollout and launch date.
- 6s: official engine-fire footage reframes the story.
- 13s: two FAA findings appear as an equal comparison.
- 26.5s: a second official vehicle-test shot resets the evidence mode.
- 29.5s: three test objectives build in sequence.
- 38s: official archived launch footage resets attention.
- 44s: the payoff connects repeatable recovery to the lunar architecture.

## What The Video Adds

- Replaces generic launch hype with three observable success criteria.
- Separates the FAA's verified findings from SpaceX's current mission goals.
- Demonstrates a repeatable, source-labelled news-Short format with a recorded
  rights review lane.

## Banned Phrases

- game changer
- insane
- Elon just changed everything
- guaranteed success
- copyright free
- fair use because it is short
- in today's video
- let's dive in

## Ending

`The spectacle gets the click. Repeatability gets Starship to work.`
