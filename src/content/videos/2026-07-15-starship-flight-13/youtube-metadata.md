## Title

Starship Flight 13: The Launch Isn't the Real Test

## Description

Starship Flight 13 may deliver another spectacular launch. But the real test is
whether the repaired system can repeat three specific operations.

SpaceX is targeting July 16, 2026. This short explains the FAA's two most
probable causes from Flight 12, the four corrective actions, and the three
Flight 13 objectives that matter most.

KEY TAKEAWAYS

- Flight 13 is a test of repeatable capability, not simply launch spectacle.
- The FAA cited heat effects and erroneous engine-alarm settings as the two
  most probable causes of the Flight 12 booster loss.
- Watch the boostback and splashdown, 20 Starlink V3 deployments, and an
  in-space Raptor relight.
