# Video Treatment

## Reference deconstruction

Primary reference: `https://www.youtube.com/shorts/2ewTt816Ca8`

- Borrow: immediate real-world footage, compact headline hierarchy, narration
  as the story spine, frequent evidence changes, and a short concluding idea.
- Avoid: copying the source channel's graphics, using broadcast compilations,
  or treating public availability as permission.

## Selected direction — official footage, light editorial explanation

- Visual spine: warm ivory canvas, paper-white source cards, charcoal type,
  and one burnt-orange signal color. Dark green is excluded.
- Scene mix: four muted SpaceX official excerpts, one equal two-cause
  comparison, one three-objective sequence, and one quiet conclusion.
- Evidence/explanation/emphasis balance: official footage carries the hook and
  visual resets; owned Remotion layouts carry the FAA findings and mission
  objectives; the end card carries the payoff.
- Narration: Aaron's approved English ElevenLabs PVC profile.
- Music: `Prism Pulse`, a restrained locally generated 42-second cue. It is a
  local-prototype candidate pending commercial-terms verification.
- Signature opportunity: none. The memorable moment should be the real engine
  fire, not a synthetic effect.
- Tradeoff: this is faster and more credible than generated atmosphere, but the
  SpaceX clips remain in a documented commentary-review lane rather than an
  automatic rights-clearance lane.

removed so no third-party music or spoken audio enters the master.
