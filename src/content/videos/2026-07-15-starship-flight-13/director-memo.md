# Director Memo

This film answers one question: what should a viewer watch after the rocket
leaves the pad?

It opens on the current Booster 20 rollout so the first frame is already real,
timely, and specific. Engine-fire footage then creates the reframe: ignition is
the spectacle, but recovery, deployment, and in-space relight are the test. The
FAA findings move onto an owned, light editorial comparison so factual causes
are not buried under generic B-roll. Three mission objectives build as a short
checklist. A final archived launch excerpt resets attention before the ending
connects repeatability to the larger lunar architecture.

The source clips are never decorative. Each is short, muted, source-labelled,
and tied to the narration's actual subject. The first two come from current
Flight 13 preparation; the final one is explicitly labelled as Starship test
archive so it cannot be mistaken for footage of Flight 13 after launch.

The ivory theme, paper surfaces, charcoal type, and restrained burnt-orange
signal consolidate the light look Aaron preferred. Dark green, generic AI
visuals, and synthetic space imagery are excluded.
