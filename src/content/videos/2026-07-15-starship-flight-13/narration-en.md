# Starship Flight 13: The Three Tests That Matter

## [HOOK]

SpaceX is targeting July sixteenth for Starship Flight thirteen. But the launch is not the real test. The question is whether SpaceX fixed the booster failure from Flight twelve.

## [SLIDE: Three tests — narration]

The FAA identified two likely causes: heat affecting propulsion components, and incorrect engine-alarm settings. SpaceX made four corrective actions. Now watch three things: a complete booster boostback burn, deployment of twenty Starlink V3 satellites, and a Raptor relight in space. If those work, Flight thirteen does more than make another spectacular video. It reopens the path toward catching both stages, and toward the Starship architecture NASA needs for future lunar missions.
