# Starship Flight 13: The Three Tests That Matter

Format: 9:16 narrated news explainer with source-labelled official footage.

## [HOOK]

SpaceX is targeting July sixteenth for Starship Flight thirteen. But the launch
is not the real test. The question is whether SpaceX fixed the booster failure
from Flight twelve.

## [SLIDE: What failed — official footage]

The FAA identified two likely causes: heat affecting propulsion components,
and incorrect engine-alarm settings. SpaceX made four corrective actions.

## [SLIDE: What to watch — three objectives]

Now watch three things: a complete booster boostback burn, deployment of twenty
Starlink V3 satellites, and a Raptor relight in space.

## [SLIDE: Why it matters — official archive]

If those work, Flight thirteen does more than make another spectacular video.
It reopens the path toward catching both stages, and toward the Starship
architecture NASA needs for future lunar missions.
