## Reference deconstruction

- Borrow: compact story scope, source-backed screen evidence, numbered process

## Selected direction — narrated light editorial tutorial

- Visual spine: pale beige canvas, warm white paper cards, charcoal type, a
  restrained warm-red signal color, two short licensed real-world inserts, and
  one real official-source screenshot.
- Scene mix: hook, phone-scanning action, principle comparison, laptop research
  action, official source, topic approval gate, production workflow,
  publishing gate, final principle.
- Narration: Aaron's ElevenLabs PVC voice, with separate English and Chinese
  localized performances rather than mixed languages in one Short.
- Music: one minimalist instrumental bed, mixed far below narration.
- Signature device: the paper approval gate makes the boundary between system
  execution and human judgment physically legible.
- Tradeoff: the format is inexpensive and repeatable, but its quality depends
  heavily on precise copy, credible sources, voice performance, and typography.

## Localization strategy

The English and Chinese films share the same factual structure and design
grammar, while each script is written for natural pacing in its own language.
Scene boundaries are measured from the actual generated narration rather than
copied between versions.

## Prototype slices

- English: 42 seconds, nine scenes.
- Chinese: 44.5 seconds, nine scenes.
- Inspect title, both licensed inserts, comparison, official source, both
  approval gates, four-step workflow, and final statement in each language.
