# Director Memo

The film's job is to make one boundary visible: automation may own repeated
execution, but Aaron keeps topic selection and publication judgment.

The visual language is intentionally light and editorial. A warm off-white
canvas and paper-white cards create continuity with the original cover Aaron
preferred. Charcoal carries the information hierarchy; warm red is reserved
for movement, verification, and approval. Dark green is excluded from the
system.

The story begins with the emotional cost of restarting the same work every
morning, then makes that repetition physical through a brief phone-scanning
insert. It reframes a content factory as repetition removal rather than
judgment removal. A second real-world insert shows source research; the official
source that follows makes the scheduled-task claim concrete. Two approval cards
interrupt the system exactly where a human decision is required. The final card
resolves the thesis: the machine keeps moving; Aaron keeps the decisions that
matter.

Narration is now the timing spine. English and Chinese are separate localized
films so each version can sound natural and each visual beat can land against
its real speech duration. Music remains subordinate and should never compete
with the voice.

The source screenshot is the only factual visual evidence. Both Pexels inserts
are explicitly labelled as licensed illustrative B-roll; they make actions
visible but do not prove claims. All other geometry is clearly editorial
explanation. No scene should imply that content is published without a final
review.
