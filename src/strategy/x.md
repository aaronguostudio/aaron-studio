## Reply Strategy (The #1 Growth Hack at 0 Followers)

This is the most important section. At your stage, **replying to others will grow you faster than your own posts.**

### Why It Works
- Your reply shows up under a big account's post â†’ their audience sees you
- A great reply gets likes â†’ X shows your profile to those people
- The big account notices you â†’ potential relationship, retweet, collaboration
- Replies are weighted **27x more than likes** in X's algorithm â€” engaging in replies trains X to show you to more people

### Who to Reply To (Build a Target List)

**Tier 1 â€” AI/Product Leaders (10K-500K followers):**
Find 15-20 accounts in the AI + product leadership space. People like:
- AI builders sharing workflows
- Product leaders writing about execution
- Solopreneurs building in public
- AI tool creators and reviewers

**Tier 2 â€” Peers (1K-10K followers):**
Find 10-15 accounts at a similar stage. These become your network â€” they'll engage back.

### How to Reply (Quality > Quantity)

**DO:**
- Add a new insight or angle they didn't cover
- Share a personal experience that reinforces their point
- Respectfully disagree with a reason
- Ask a genuinely thoughtful question
- "I tried this and here's what happened..."

**DON'T:**
- "Great post!" (zero value, looks desperate)
- Just restate what they said
- Drop your link unprompted
- Reply to everything â€” be selective

**Examples of great replies:**

> *Their post:* "AI is changing how product teams work"
> *Bad reply:* "So true! ðŸ”¥"
> *Good reply:* "We shifted our entire sprint planning to AI-assisted specs 3 months ago. Biggest surprise: the junior PMs adapted faster than seniors. The skill gap isn't technical â€” it's willingness to let go of the old process."

> *Their post:* "Here's my content creation workflow"
> *Good reply:* "I built something similar but added a step â€” AI generates platform-specific versions (X thread, LinkedIn, newsletter) from one source doc. Cuts my content time from 8hrs/week to 2hrs. Happy to share the architecture."

### The 30-Minute Daily Routine

1. **First 10 min:** Check your target list. Find 2-3 posts worth replying to.
2. **Next 15 min:** Write thoughtful replies (2-4 sentences each, not one-liners).
3. **Last 5 min:** Check your own notifications, respond to comments on your posts.

**Track your target accounts in a simple list. Rotate through them so you're not always replying to the same 3 people.**

## Content Rules

1. **Never post a naked link.** Always create native content first. Link goes in a reply.
2. **Every thread must stand alone.** If nobody clicks the blog link, they still got value.
3. **Hook in the first tweet.** You have 1 second to stop the scroll. Lead with outcome or surprise.
4. **End threads with a CTA.** Rotate between: follow, newsletter, reply/engage.
5. **One idea per thread.** Don't try to cover everything. Go deep on one thing.
6. **Use "I" not "you should."** Share what you did, not what others should do. It's more authentic and less preachy.
7. **Include a visual when possible.** Screenshots, diagrams, before/after â€” images boost engagement.

### Hook Formulas That Work
- "I [did surprising thing]. Here's what happened:"
- "Most [role] get [topic] wrong. Here's why:"
- "[Number] lessons from [specific experience]:"
- "I spent [time] building [thing]. Here's the breakdown:"
- "Stop [common mistake]. Do this instead:"

## Algorithm Cheat Sheet

**What X rewards:**
- Native content (no external links in main post)
- Replies and conversations (27x weight vs likes)
- Dwell time (people stopping to read)
- Bookmarks (strong quality signal)
- Consistency (regular posting schedule)
- Premium subscribers get a boost

**What X penalizes:**
- External links (~50% reach reduction)
- Excessive posting without engagement
- Follow/unfollow games
- Being muted/blocked by users
- Posting and ghosting (not replying to comments)

**Content reach hierarchy:**
1. Native video (especially long-form)
2. Images with text
3. Threads
4. Single text tweets
5. X Articles
6. Quote tweets
7. Polls
