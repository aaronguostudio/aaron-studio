## What We Have Done

- Met customers at their office — they have a 4-story office and a complete team for the gaming industry
- We met 5 of their decision-maker level representatives and had some good discussions
- The 5 of them represent 4 companies behind the organization
- So far, Messi is the most interested one
- Raymond also asked some good questions
- Aaron started with the presentation and had a live demo for the products and key modules
- Noted that Janet has a very good relationship with them — this becomes the good entry point for us, but having a solid, powerful product is the key to start business with them

They asked a lot of questions around "Initiative" module, "Capacity Allocation", "AI Agents" etc. Their questions are very spot-on regarding the pain point for people on how to allocate people's time, tracking performance, etc.

We were able to meet the CEO of the whole organization during dinner. It's a very good sign that we are building a strong relationship with them.

## Meeting Notes

### Integration

- Integration could be the most important blocker — how to integrate with existing systems e.g. Jira, Notion etc.
- Raymond mentioned that it was a lot of training when onboarding Jira. So how to effectively integrate with our tool is something we should think about.
- Messi also emphasizes having smooth integration with Jira could make this product successful.

### AI Agents

- AI agents has been mentioned many times. I think it showed interest when I mentioned our "Digital Twin" and "Marketplace" concept.
- OpenClaw is another buzz word that has been mentioned. Messi mentioned that teaching users to learn the UI and make it right is harder than just talking to OpenClaw — if we can integrate with that, it would be great.
- Note: they don't allow installing OpenClaw on their machines yet.

### Value & Audience

- Messi mentioned he can see the value of OrgNext's initiatives module if we can calculate how much cost is spent on different projects. Basically the capacity report using FTE and rate data.
- Who is the audience is another question. Everyone can see this is a tool that benefits managers, but how the IC will use the tool is something that has been asked.
- Also we should focus on one key area and make it great — it can really address people's pain point.

## Summary

Overall the meeting went successfully. The customers asked many spot-on questions that we are trying to solve. Janet will follow up with them and see what's the next step.

We can feel that it's not easy to sell OrgNext without integrating with existing workflow. For brand new users, which we might talk to the next couple days, could be a better candidate. This is not a surprise. OrgNext is a tool that will change how people work, and changing that habit is never easy. This makes us consider what Jon mentioned before — how can we let AI connect to the different systems and do the work. This is also similar to what Palantir does. We should really look into this as a primary product focus.

AI agents is something we should double down on. It doesn't mean we did wrong — we are building the foundation to get the system robust, make the data model accurate, make the workflow work. But all this foundation work should benefit AI to make a more proactive and easy-to-use system. I can see the future of software is coming — will we be the ones to catch it?

Founding a business is not easy. There are ups and downs, there are learnings, there are failures. But now is the best time to run a business, way easier and better than before. I can see the future as long as we stick together as a team.
