**Date:** 2026-07-17

**Date:** 2026-07-17  
**Decision:** Optimize for attributable visits and reading-depth signals rather than pageviews alone.  
**Why:** Current analytics contain local traffic and suspiciously shallow high-volume visits.

## D-007 — Separate Concept Supply From Distribution Cadence

**Date:** 2026-07-18  
**Decision:** The content employee may grow the Learn library continuously, while
the distribution employee independently selects concepts on an initial 48-hour
minimum cadence.  
**Why:** Library depth is an owned-content asset; social frequency is an audience
decision. Coupling them would either slow the library or turn Aaron's feeds into
a content dump.

## D-008 — Use Deterministic Concept Cards

**Date:** 2026-07-18  
**Decision:** Build cards from a reusable layout and a small concept-specific
visual primitive rather than generating an unrelated AI illustration each time.  
**Why:** A deterministic system preserves Aaron's visual identity, supports
automation, and makes the meaning of the image auditable.

## D-009 — Keep Distribution Cards English-First and Visually Singular

**Date:** 2026-07-18  
**Decision:** Use one audience language per social asset. For the current
English-first audience, use the `sleek-dark` system: a solid background,
high-contrast typography, and one concept-specific visual primitive.  
**Why:** Repeating the title in two languages weakens hierarchy on a small
social canvas. The bilingual Learn library remains available at the destination;
the card should optimize the first impression for the selected channel.

## D-010 — Stop at Pilot End and Await Renewal

**Date:** 2026-07-20  
**Decision:** Set the brand-sales employee state to `awaiting_renewal` after the
four-day final review.  
**Why:** The contract's pilot stop rule requires the role to stop autonomous
external action after 2026-07-20 unless Aaron renews or changes the mandate.
`A-001` was not approved during the pilot, so there is no calibrated public
distribution authority to carry forward.
