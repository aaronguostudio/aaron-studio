The Law of Attraction has always worked for me.

So I built an AI system that does it — programmatically.

Here's how Signal works:

---

Every major outcome in my life followed the same pattern.

Opening a music instrument store in university — no capital, no plan. Just obsession. I read about entrepreneurship constantly, surrounded myself with people building things. Months later: I was running a business.

Immigrating to Canada. Becoming a partner at a finance firm.

None of it was a straight line. But all of it followed the same loop:

Intense focus → Immersion → A series of "coincidences" that lined up → Action.

I didn't just want things. I saturated myself in the world of what I wanted. And then things found me.

---

That's not magic. There's a mechanism.

Your brain's Reticular Activating System (RAS) processes roughly 11 million bits of information per second. Your conscious mind handles about 40.

The RAS is the filter. It decides what reaches awareness and what gets ignored.

When you set a clear intention, you reprogram the filter. Suddenly you notice the article that was always there. The person you should have met months ago. The opportunity sitting in plain sight.

The Law of Attraction is a biological recommendation engine.

And like every recommendation engine, it has a hard ceiling — bandwidth, processing speed, the finite hours you can read and connect and explore.

AI doesn't have those limitations.

---

So I built Signal.

It monitors Hacker News, Reddit, ArXiv, X, Product Hunt, GitHub Trending, and more — 24/7.

Every item gets scored by an LLM against a profile of my interests, goals, and blind spots. High-scoring items hit my morning digest. Everything else is available but deprioritized.

Every hour: rescan. Every morning: deliver.

What this actually does:
- I "read" 500+ items a day through AI summaries — then decide which ones deserve full attention
- No doomscrolling. No algorithmic distraction designed for platform engagement, not my growth
- Lucky coincidences multiplied — papers I wouldn't have found, people I wouldn't have known to look for
- The sharper I define my interests, the sharper the signal gets

---

This creates a flywheel.

Signal → deeper knowledge → more informed action → new connections → better Signal.

A concrete example: Signal started surfacing world model research in late 2025. I went deep. I started writing about world model applications in ways that weren't common yet. That positioned me in conversations I wouldn't have been part of otherwise. Those conversations fed new sources back into Signal.

This is engineered serendipity — designing the conditions where valuable accidents happen more often.

---

The tech world calls this the shift from the Attention Economy to the Intention Economy.

The attention economy (social media as we know it) optimizes for platform engagement. It captures your focus and sells it.

The intention economy is different: systems that optimize for your goals, not the platform's metrics.

Researchers at Cambridge are already warning that AI will soon intercept your developing intentions in real time and sell them to advertisers. The arms race is coming either way.

The question is whether you build your own system — or let someone else build one for you.

---

I didn't stop believing in the Law of Attraction. I just decided to engineer it.

The questions I'd ask:
- What are you trying to attract into your life right now?
- What information would you need to see — consistently, every day — to get there?
- What if you stopped leaving that to an algorithm that doesn't know you, and built one that does?

You don't need to believe in the Law of Attraction. You just need to believe in better signal processing.
