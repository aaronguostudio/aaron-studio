# I Engineered the Law of Attraction with AI

## [HOOK]

Every morning, before I open my phone, an AI delivers a digest of the most relevant things happening in my world. Papers, projects, threads — each scored for how much they matter to me right now. I built this system because I believe in the Law of Attraction. And I wanted a better version of it.

## [SLIDE: I Built a Better Law of Attraction — 00-cover.png]

I call it Signal. It monitors Hacker News, Reddit, ArXiv, X, Product Hunt, GitHub Trending — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Every item gets scored by AI against a profile of my interests, goals, and blind spots. High-scoring items hit my inbox every morning. Everything else I can skim or skip.

[IMAGE: s01-01-morning-inbox.png]

This system runs while I sleep. It runs while I'm in meetings. It never stops. And the result is something that used to feel like magic — suddenly feeling very much like engineering.

I read about entrepreneurship, talked about it constantly, surrounded myself with people who were building things. A few months later, I was running a music instrument store with friends on campus.

[IMAGE: s02-02-immigration.png]

[IMAGE: s02-03-finance.png]

## [SLIDE: The Mechanism Behind LOA — s03-01-loa-four-steps.png]

Strip away the mysticism, and the mechanism is actually pretty clear.

Step one: intention. You define what matters to you — a goal, a direction, a domain. Step two: attention. You expose yourself to the right information and the right people. Step three: pattern recognition. Your brain starts filtering the world for relevant signals. And step four: action. You act on the coincidences that aren't really coincidences at all.

[IMAGE: 04-infographic-ras-mechanism.png]

There's real neuroscience behind step three. Your brain's Reticular Activating System — the RAS — processes roughly 11 million bits of information per second. But your conscious mind handles only about 40. The RAS is the filter. It decides what reaches your awareness and what gets ignored.

When you set a clear intention, you reprogram that filter. Suddenly you notice the article that was always there. The person you should have met months ago. The opportunity sitting in plain sight. This is not magic. This is signal acquisition.

---

## [SLIDE: LOA Is a Recommendation Engine — 01-comparison-loa-vs-algorithm.png]

And here's the part that really clicked for me. This is exactly how a recommendation algorithm works.

Set an intention — that's defining a user profile. Expose yourself to information — that's ingesting a content feed. The RAS filters for relevance — that's a ranking model scoring content. And the coincidences surface — that's your personalized feed appearing.

[IMAGE: s04-01-bandwidth-limit.png]

The Law of Attraction is a biological recommendation engine. And like every recommendation engine, it has a hard ceiling. Bandwidth. Processing speed. The finite hours you can read and connect and explore.

AI doesn't have those limitations.

## [SLIDE: How Signal Works — 02-framework-signal-architecture.png]

So in early 2025 I started learning AI seriously. And one theme kept surfacing — the best researchers were building systems that model what matters, not just generate text. Yann LeCun left Meta to build AMI Labs. Fei-Fei Li raised at a five billion dollar valuation for World Labs. The direction was clear.

I wasn't trying to build AGI. I was trying to build a better filter for myself. That's when Signal came together.

[IMAGE: s05-01-signal-scoring.png]

The architecture is simple. Sources feed in from Hacker News, Reddit, ArXiv, X, Product Hunt, GitHub Trending. Every item gets evaluated by an LLM against my personal interest profile — my current focus areas, the questions I'm trying to answer, the blind spots I want to address. High-scoring items are must-reads. Everything else is available but deprioritized.

[IMAGE: s05-02-morning-digest.png]

Every hour it rescans. Every morning it delivers. What this actually does is expand my attention bandwidth roughly a hundred times. I process over 500 items a day through AI summaries. I decide which ones deserve full attention. No doomscrolling. No algorithmic distraction designed for platform engagement, not my growth.

## [SLIDE: From Attention Economy to Intention Economy — 05-comparison-attention-vs-intention.png]

This is what the tech world is starting to call the shift from the Attention Economy to the Intention Economy.

The attention economy — social media as we know it — optimizes for platform engagement. It captures your focus and sells it. The intention economy is different: systems that optimize for your goals, not the platform's metrics.

[IMAGE: s06-01-cambridge-warning.png]

Researchers at Cambridge are already warning that AI will soon intercept your developing intentions in real time and sell them to advertisers. The arms race is coming either way. The only question is whether you build your own system — or let someone else build one for you.

## [SLIDE: The Signal Flywheel — 03-flowchart-signal-flywheel.png]

The traditional Law of Attraction is passive. Set your intention and wait for the universe to deliver. That framing always bothered me. It leaves too much to chance.

The AI-augmented version is an active flywheel.

Signal captures what matters in your domain. Knowledge deepens as you process curated information every day. Action becomes more informed — better decisions, sharper writing, the right conversations. And then Signal improves, because your actions generate new connections, new sources, new feedback.

[IMAGE: s07-01-world-model-research.png]

A concrete example: Signal started surfacing world model research in late 2025. I went deep on it. I started writing about world model applications in ways that weren't common yet. That positioned me in conversations I wouldn't have been part of otherwise. Those conversations fed new sources back into Signal.

This is engineered serendipity — designing the conditions where valuable accidents happen more often.
