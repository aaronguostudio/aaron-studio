## Part 2: What the Law of Attraction Actually Is (Reframed)

Strip away the mysticism. What's left?

The Law of Attraction, mechanistically, is:
1. **Intention** → You define what matters to you (a goal, a domain, a direction)
2. **Attention** → You expose yourself to relevant information and people
3. **Pattern Recognition** → Your brain (reticular activating system) starts filtering the world for relevant signals
4. **Action** → You act on those signals — the "coincidences" that aren't coincidences

This is literally how a **recommendation algorithm** works:
1. Define preferences (user profile)
2. Ingest data (content feed)
3. Score relevance (ranking model)
4. Surface what matters (personalized feed)

Thesis: **The Law of Attraction is a biological recommendation engine. AI lets you build a better one.**

Bridge from personal realization to AI learning journey:

Key line: "Your brain is a great signal processor. But it has bandwidth limits. AI doesn't."

## Part 4: Building Signal — My Programmatic Law of Attraction

The technical story (kept accessible):

- Built "Signal" — an AI-curated intelligence system that runs 24/7
- It monitors: HN, Reddit, ArXiv, X/Twitter, Product Hunt, GitHub Trending, Lobsters
- Every item gets an **AI relevance score** based on a personal profile (my interests, my goals, my blind spots)
- Items scored 9-10 are "must reads" — these include world model research, AI + finance intersection, agentic AI breakthroughs
- Every morning: AI-generated daily digest arrives in my inbox — a personalized intelligence briefing
- Every hour: the system rescans, re-scores, surfaces what's new

What this actually does:
- **Expands my attention bandwidth** by 100x — I "read" 500+ articles/day through AI summaries
- **Eliminates noise** — no more doomscrolling, no more algorithm-driven distractions
- **Creates "lucky coincidences"** — I find papers, people, and ideas I would never have encountered organically
- **Compounds over time** — the more I refine my profile, the sharper the signal gets

Real example: Through Signal, I discovered [specific paper/project/person] that directly led to [specific outcome]. This would have taken months of manual browsing — or never happened at all.

## Part 5: The Flywheel — Signal → Knowledge → Action → Signal

This is where it gets interesting:

The traditional Law of Attraction is passive: "think positive thoughts and good things come."
The AI-augmented version is an **active flywheel**:

1. **Signal** captures what matters in your domain
2. **Knowledge** deepens as you process curated information daily
3. **Action** becomes more informed — you make better bets, write sharper content, connect with the right people
4. **Signal** improves because your actions generate new data, new connections, new feedback

This is not manifestation. This is **engineered serendipity**.

Concrete example of the flywheel in action:
- Signal surfaced world model research → I started studying it deeply → I began positioning as someone who understands world model applications → New conversations opened → Those conversations fed back into Signal as new sources

## Part 6: Why This Matters Now

- We're entering the age of **information abundance + attention scarcity**
- The average person sees 10,000+ pieces of content/day — and retains almost none
- Traditional social media algorithms optimize for engagement (their goals), not for growth (your goals)
- The people who win in the next decade will be the ones who **control their own information diet**

"Everyone has access to GPT. But not everyone has built a system that uses AI to serve their specific intentions, 24/7, while they sleep."

This is the real competitive advantage: not AI as a tool, but AI as an **extension of your cognitive architecture**.
